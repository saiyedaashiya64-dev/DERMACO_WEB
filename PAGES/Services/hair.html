Understanding the Hair Growth Cycle

To effectively manage hair loss, it is important to understand how hair grows naturally. Hair growth follows a structured cycle, and disruption in any phase of this cycle can result in increased hair fall, thinning, or reduced hair density.

Each hair strand goes through multiple growth stages throughout its life. Factors such as genetics, hormonal imbalance, stress, illness, and nutritional deficiencies can disturb this cycle, leading to visible hair concerns.

Anagen Phase (Growth Phase)

This is the active growth phase, lasting between 2 to 7 years. During this phase, hair grows continuously at an average rate of about half an inch per month. A healthy anagen phase is essential for strong and thick hair.

Catagen Phase (Transition Phase)

The catagen phase is a short transition period that lasts around 10 days. During this stage, hair stops growing and detaches from the blood supply, preparing for the resting phase.

Telogen Phase (Resting Phase)

In the telogen phase, hair remains in the follicle without active growth. Around 10‚Äì15% of hair is usually in this phase at any given time. This phase lasts approximately 3 months.

Exogen Phase (Shedding Phase)

The exogen phase marks the natural shedding of hair strands. Old hair falls out to make space for new hair growth, allowing the cycle to begin again.

  • Anagen: Active growth phase
  • Catagen: Transition phase
  • Telogen: Resting phase
  • Exogen: Natural shedding
