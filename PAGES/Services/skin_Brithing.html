Understanding Skin Pigmentation

Pigmentation refers to the coloring of the skin caused by melanin. There are two main types of pigmentation‚Äîhyperpigmentation, which appears as dark patches, and hypopigmentation, which appears as light patches on the skin.

While pigmentation is not harmful, it can significantly affect skin appearance and confidence if left untreated.

The Role of Melanin

Melanin is produced by melanocytes and determines the color of our skin. It also acts as a natural defense against harmful UV radiation. When skin is exposed to sunlight, melanin production increases, leading to tanning or darkened patches.

Excess melanin production can result in uneven skin tone, dark spots, and long-term pigmentation concerns.

Sun Exposure and Skin Pigmentation

Sun exposure is one of the primary causes of pigmentation. UVA rays penetrate deep into the skin, leading to premature aging and pigmentation, while UVB rays affect the surface, causing sunburn and discoloration.

Prolonged exposure can result in sunspots, uneven skin tone, and premature aging. Regular sunscreen use is essential to protect the skin both indoors and outdoors.

Hormonal Influences on Skin Pigmentation

Hormonal changes during pregnancy, puberty, menopause, or due to birth control pills can cause melasma‚Äîa form of pigmentation that appears as dark patches on the face.

Managing hormonal pigmentation requires professional evaluation and a personalised treatment approach under dermatologist supervision.
